Window 1.0

The window 1.0 was shown to the public 2 years early but it was on November 20, 1985, the Microsoft window company release the window 1.0 in the United States. window 1.0 was delayed due to the design of the program. Microsoft from the early 80s wanted to create a Graphical user interface, a device that allowed the user to use icons to connect to the electronic (GUI). This was the first big start of the window computer. The lead developer of window 1.0 is Scoot A. McGregor, the role given by Bill Gates, also known as the co-founder of Microsoft. With the help of Compaq, zenith, and DEC company the window 1.0 can run on MS-DOS. During that time many computers needed Unix, however, window did not use Unix to create their IMB PC, making it more unique than other pc. At that time, the window 1.0 can perform many features like a calculator, notepad, mini-games, paint, and clock. On the window, you can use a mouse or without the mouse; you can click and drag. window 1.0 cost $99 at that time when it was out. Some reason window 1.0 was a favorite by people was memory management, downloading a file, or sending files. In the program of window 1.0 , there was even an easter egg that showed the developers. And with the support of other companies like Compaq, zenith, and DEC; it let window company get more popular over time. window 1.0 was also Microsoft's longest-supported window.
